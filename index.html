Hey there! If you're reading this, you probably want to know more about me. Otherwise, why the heck are you here? After all, this is my personal site - there's nothing to see here other than lame facts about myself!

My Story

First of all, I'd like to warn you that this is basically an auto-biography - so you don't care about me, you can probably expect it to be pretty boring. After all, people only read autobiographies of famous people, and I'm just an ordinary 19 year-old kid. I was going to try and make it intereting by making a cool and interactive timeline, but that was too much work, so instead you can enjoy some static text!

  • 1998: I burst into existence on June 2, 1998, born in Shanghai, China. Fun fact: It took me more than half a day to finally come out of the womb... poor mom!

  • 2002: In November of 2002, my family moved from China to Canada to begin a new life. Even though I did not make the decision, this is the easily single most impactful event in my life. Growing up in Canada has opened up a world of freedom and opportunity for me that I would not be able to experience in China.

  • 2006: My sister Julia is born in Mississauga, Canada.

  • 2012: I begin secondary school, taking the International Baccalaureate program. I would go on to sucessfully complete the program and receive my diploma.

  • 2016: I begin my post-secondary studies at the University of Waterloo, studying Computer Engineering (co -op).

I should also mention that my full name is Jeffrey Zhen Xuan Huang, which is where the 'zx' comes from in jzxhuang. That translates to 黃真軒 in Chinese - don't ask me what that means, because I have no clue!

My Passions

Technology

From a young age, I have been fascinated by technology and all the wonderful things it can do. At the age of 12, I built my first PC - a huge accomplishment for me at the time - and I haven't looked back since. Today, I like buliding all sorts of things that I find cool and interesting as I look to build my career in the computing field (check out my projects for more).

To me, technology is powerful because it connects us and motivates us. Our wonderful smart devices allow us to connect with our loved ones in ways that were previously unthinkable. Perhaps more incredible is how we are able to use technology to make the world a better place. From space exploration to the smartphone to electric vehicles, human innovation is truly limitless, and I can't wait to see what the future holds.

Travel and Adventure

My life bucket list is pretty much to travel the world and visit as many places as I can. I can thank my parents for my love of travelling. Every year, they plan a trip to a new city or country, and I get to tag along for the ride. For me, travelling is all about experiencing the unique landscapes, cultures, foods, and people of the world. I'm proud to say I have set foot in nearly all the provinces and territories in the beautiful country of Canada, and I hope to continue the travel the Earth (and perhaps beyond) and experience the many wonders that the world has to offer.

Another thing I love about travelling is the thrill of adventure, and one thing that gives me that same thrill is snowboarding. The excitement of bombing down the hill with snow spraying in your face is second to none. Although I have only been able to snowboard on Canadian turf, I'm proud to say that I am a professionally qualified snowboard instructor. One day, I hope to visit and conquer the greatest mountains from all around the globe.

Don't get me wrong though, I'm not one of those picky food connoisseurs. I love amazing food, but I'll settle for anything that tastes good, even if it's short of perfect. It doesn't have to be fancy or expensive - some Kraft Dinner gets the job done when you're in a pinch!

Oh, and I'll eat pretty much anything. As long as it's been proven to be edible, and tastes good to me, I'll eat it!

GameMaker

Using GameMaker Studio, I helped design and code two games, titled Jester's Rampage and Mansion Buster. Both games placed top 5 in the DPCDSB Gamemaker competition.

Jester's Rampage

This is the the first real project I ever worked on as part of a team, and one that I am still very proud of to this day. Jester's Rampage is a medieval themed side-scroller featuring a jester who is looking for revenge on the king and queen. At this time, I prety much didn't know how to code at all, so it was perfect that GameMaker has a drag-and-drop interface that made it easy to use. As crappy as that may sound, we still managed to implement features like basic AI, cutscenes, sound effects, and more.

This game was my first taste of working in a team-based environment, and opened my eyes to how many different roles are needed to complete a project. From art to music to level design, it took a huge team effort and hard work from everyone involved to complete the game. The importance of good organization and collaboration was crucial in order to complete the project.

You can check out the trailer I made for the game below, and download the game if you want to try it out!

Mansion Buster

Mansion Buster features a rookie ghost hunter who takes on a mansion that has been haunted for centuries. As this was the second game I worked on, the team had much more experience after our work with Jester's Rampage. The game code was written almost completely without drag-and-drop elements, and kept the same great artwork and music from Jester's Rampage while improving on the game mechanics.

We were much more efficient this time around, and implemented many more levels and puzzles into the game. I never got around to finishing a trailer for this game, but you can try it out for yourself if you'd like.

UW NanoRobotics

The University of Waterloo NanoRobotics Group (UWNRG) is an undergraduate design team that builds innovative robots at the nano and micro level. Despite being the only team consisting of only undergrads to compete, the team has won the ICRA Mobile Microrobotics Challenge twice in the past 5 years. I am part of two of the group's many teams. Find out more at the UWNRG website.

Solenoid Actuated Microbot (SAM)

SAM is the team's current compeition robot. It uses five solenoids which can be individually pulsed using PWM modulation to provide precise, omni-directional movement to a tiny, uniformly shaped magnet. As part of SAM, I have been primarly responsible for the circut design used to control the robot, as well as bridging communications between the hardware and software team.

Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW)

SAW is the team's first completely fluid nanobot design. It utilizes the reverse-piezoelectric effect to convert electrical energy into Rayleigh surface waves which propel a nano-droplet across a lithium niobate wafer. I have been responsible for the majority of the SAW circuit design, which requires a high-frequency amplifying circuit to generate the necessary waves. I have also been involved in the theory, prototyping, and testing of the robot.

Object Tracking Car

This project was started with a group of friends, and is currently nicknamed Claybotics (for no particular reason). We are building a car that is able to identify an object and follow it on its own. It will be able to determine how far away it is from the object (depth) as well as which side the object is on relative to the car (horizontal). The car will also be stop in case a foreign object is in front of it.

This project is being built in Python using OpenCV, and is currently a work in progress. You can find a photo of the car in its elementary stages below, or follow the project on Github.
